Question 1

Who was the first Asian American woman elected to the U.S. Congress?

Answer

Patsy Mink was elected in 1964 and was the first woman of color in Congress. She co-authored Title IX, which prohibits gender discrimination in education. Mink was a third-generation Japanese American, having been born and raised on the island of Maui.

Question 2

In which U.S. Movement was the term "Asian American" coined?

Answer

The term Asian American was created in 1968 to unify diverse Asian ethnic groups under a shared identity for political activism.

Question 3

Which U.S. state has the highest percentage of Asian American residents?

Answer

Over 50% of Hawaii's population is of Asian descent, with large communities of Japanese, Filipino, and Chinese heritage.

Question 4

Which Asian cuisine was the first to be recognized by UNESCO as an intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity?

Answer

"Washoku" the traditional dietary culture of Japan, was recognized by UNESCO in 2013 for promoting social cohesion and respect for nature.

Question 5

Which ancient civilization developed the concept of zero?

Answer

The concept of zero was developed by ancient Indian mathematicians around the 5th century CE. This revolutionary mathematical concept was later transmitted to the Islamic world and then to Europe, fundamentally changing mathematics.
